Ocean Observation

There are two ways to access ocean observation in the Ocean Navigator: global in-situ observation from global databases and CLASS4.

Global Ocean

Observation Ocean observation from various platforms will be available to compare with ocean prediction systems. This includes ARGO, glider, drifters, tagged animals and oceanographic missions. Currently, we are providing 2022 ARGO data from the IFREMER database.


CLASS4 Datasets

There are various global ocean prediction systems available to describe the physical and biological environments. The information from these systems are predicted estimates of the environmental variables which are valuable for users of the ocean. It is important to have metrics in place to validate these estimates. In physical oceanography there have been several metrics developed including the CLASS 4 metric. CLASS4 are created when forecast and analysis fields are interpolated to in-situ ocean observations at particular locations.

We have two CLASS4 datasets available in the Ocean Navigator: OceanPredict and RIOPS Assimilated Observation. The OceanPredict CLASS4 is generated daily. Participating parties contribute by adding the model equivalent to an ocean observation for variables collected at a particular location. The CLASS4 file is then made available on the USGODAE website. The RIOPS Assimilated Observation data are based on the data assimilated into the Regional Ice Ocean Prediction System 1/12Â° (RIOPS). The in situ ocean observations are a result of the QC and thinning process applied to in situ data from CORIOLIS. A description of the data product can be found on the CMEMS website (reference below).
