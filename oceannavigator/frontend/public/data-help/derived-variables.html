Derived Acoustic Variables





Speed of Sound [m/s]

The speed of sound is the distance [meter] travelled per unit of time [second] by a sound wave as it propagates through water.


Sonic layer Depth [m]

Depth of the maximum speed of sound that is higher in the water column than the critical depth. No value where deep sound channel axis does not exist.


Sound Channel Axis [m]

Depth of the global minimum in the speed of sound. No value if minimum is at the surface.


Critical Depth [m]

Second depth where the speed of sound is equal to the speed at the sonic layer depth. In the application we used a nearest neighbour interpolation.

Technical note: Due to computer rounding errors, this calculation will select two points within a small tolerance range.


Depth Excess [m]

Difference between the Critical Depth and the ocean bottom as specified by the dataset's bathymetry.


Potential Subsurface Sound Channel

This variable represents the potential existence of a sub-surface sound channel. Selecting this variable will yield 0 or 1, where 0 means a sub-surface sound channel does not exist, 1 means a sub-surface sound channel potentially exists.


Other Derived Variables


Potential temperature [°C]

Where source potential temperature is variable in degrees Kelvin we convert to Celsius with the following equation, C=K-273.15


Water Velocity Magnitude [m/s]

Water velocity is calculated using the variables water east velocity [m/s] and water north velocity [m/s] as input.


Water Velocity Bearing [°]

Water velocity is calculated using the variables water east velocity [m/s] and water north velocity [m/s] as input.
